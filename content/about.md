I'm Jozef, a software developer with **10+ years** of experience, focusing mostly on backend and web applications.

Most of my day happens in **Node.js** and **TypeScript**, with Postgres and AWS somewhere nearby. Over the years, **Go** went from "language I'm curious about" to the thing I reach for whenever I build my own tools — like [novus](https://github.com/jozefcipa/novus), a local HTTPS proxy, or a [USB macro button](https://github.com/jozefcipa/usb-button) running TinyGo on a Raspberry Pi Pico. In the past I also worked with PHP, jQuery, and a little bit of Python — we don't talk about the XSLT era 😅.

What started as "getting interested in **IoT**" a few years back has quietly turned into a desk full of projects: a [HomeKit thermostat](https://github.com/jozefcipa/homekit-thermostat) on an ESP32, a clock with an old-school VFD display, and a 27" iMac that I gutted and turned into an external monitor. I prefer cheap DIY solutions over expensive products — half the fun is that there's always something new to learn along the way.

When writing code, I think several steps ahead and try to predict the issues that could bite later. I'm a minimalist with an eye for detail, always **curious about how things work under the hood** — one day I'd still love to take a proper look at **Linux internals** 🤓.

I'm not afraid to start things I know nothing about. **Learning by doing** is my way to go — figure it out while building it, write it down on this blog afterwards.

When I'm not coding or tinkering, I'm usually _exploring new countries_ 🌎 or _riding my motorcycle_ 🏍 — sometimes both at once, like the time I rode all the way up to Nordkapp. If you want to talk about backend systems, DIY hardware, or the best way to route cables behind a desk, you can find me on [GitHub](https://github.com/jozefcipa) or [LinkedIn](https://linkedin.com/in/jozefcipa) — or just write me at [hello@jozefcipa.com](mailto:hello@jozefcipa.com).
